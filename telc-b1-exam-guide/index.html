How to Prepare for the telc B1 German Exam

Updated April 2026 · 8 min read

The telc Deutsch B1 exam is one of the most important German language exams. Whether you need it for German citizenship, a job application, or personal goals, passing B1 proves you can communicate independently in everyday situations. This guide covers everything you need to know to prepare effectively.

What is the telc B1 Exam?

The telc Deutsch B1 is a standardized language exam recognized across Germany and Europe. It tests your ability to understand and communicate in German at an intermediate level. The exam is commonly required for German citizenship applications (Einbürgerung) and is accepted by employers and educational institutions.

Exam Structure

The telc B1 exam consists of a written part and an oral part:

  • Hören (Listening) — 25 minutes: You listen to recordings and answer multiple choice and true/false questions. Topics include announcements, phone messages, and conversations.
  • Lesen (Reading) — 45 minutes: You read texts like emails, advertisements, and articles, then answer comprehension questions.
  • Schreiben (Writing) — 30 minutes: You write a semi-formal letter or email based on a given situation, addressing specific points.
  • Sprechen (Speaking) — 15 minutes: You speak with a partner. Part 1 is self-introduction, Part 2 is a conversation about an everyday topic, and Part 3 is collaborative planning.

Scoring and Passing

Each section is scored separately. You need at least 60% in the written part (Hören + Lesen + Schreiben combined) and 60% in the oral part (Sprechen) to pass. If you fail one part, you can retake just that part without repeating the whole exam.

Preparation Tips

1. Start with Practice Exams

The best way to prepare is by taking complete practice exams under real conditions. This helps you understand the format, manage your time, and identify weak areas. Use a timer and try to simulate the real exam environment as closely as possible.

2. Focus on the Speaking Section

Many candidates neglect the speaking section because it is hard to practice alone. However, it counts for a significant portion of your score. Practice self-introductions, discussing everyday topics, and collaborative planning tasks. Using AI-powered speaking practice tools can help you prepare without a language partner.

3. Build Vocabulary by Topic

The B1 exam covers everyday topics like health, travel, work, housing, and education. Create vocabulary lists organized by topic and review them regularly. Focus on words and phrases you would actually use in conversation.

4. Practice Writing Letters

The writing section always requires a semi-formal letter. Practice common scenarios: writing to a landlord, responding to a job advertisement, making a complaint, or asking for information. Always include a greeting (Sehr geehrte Damen und Herren), address all required points, and end with a closing (Mit freundlichen Grüßen).

5. Listen to German Daily

Improve your listening skills by consuming German media every day. Podcasts like "Slow German" or "Deutsche Welle Langsam gesprochene Nachrichten" are excellent for B1 level. Start with slower content and gradually increase the speed.

Common Mistakes to Avoid

  • Not reading the instructions carefully — each section has specific requirements
  • Spending too much time on difficult questions — move on and come back later
  • Writing too little in the Schreiben section — aim for 150 words minimum
  • Not practicing the speaking section enough — this is where most people lose points
  • Ignoring time management — practice with a timer from the beginning

How Long Does Preparation Take?

Most learners need 4 to 8 weeks of focused preparation if they are already at a solid A2 level. If you are starting from A2, plan for at least 2 to 3 months of regular study. Consistency is more important than intensity — 30 minutes of daily practice is better than 3 hours once a week.

Resources for Preparation

  • Practice exams that simulate real test conditions
  • AI-powered speaking practice for the oral exam
  • German podcasts and news for listening practice
  • Letter writing templates for the Schreiben section
  • Vocabulary lists organized by exam topics

The telc B1 exam is challenging but very achievable with the right preparation. Focus on practicing all four sections equally, especially speaking, and you will be well prepared for exam day.
